During an average of 9.5 years of follow-up, 14893 major cardiovascular events including cardiogenic death, myocardial infarction, stroke and heart failure occurred. Contrastive analysis found that the more periodontal disease, caries, or tooth loss, the higher the risk of cardiovascular disease.
