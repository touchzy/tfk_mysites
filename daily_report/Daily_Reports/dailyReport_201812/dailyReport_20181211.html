Generally speaking, there are six major factors that directly affect blood pressure. Age and gender. During the development of infants to adults, blood pressure rises rapidly; in adulthood, the trend of blood pressure rises slowly, and then gradually increases with age. The climate changes from cold to hot. The prevalence of hypertension in cold areas was significantly higher than that in warm areas. Individually, blood pressure also fluctuates with the change of weather, especially in hypertensive patients. Circadian rhythm. The changes of blood pressure during the day and at night are rhythmic. Influenced by sleep and activity, blood pressure decreases during sleep and rises during activity.
